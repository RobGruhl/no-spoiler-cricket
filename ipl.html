§ Format & rules

2026 — structure

Format

Double group-stage (two groups of 5), each team plays 14 league matches. Top 4 advance to playoffs: Qualifier 1 (#1 v #2), Eliminator (#3 v #4), Qualifier 2, Final.

Impact Player

Retained for 2026 — each team can substitute one player between overs 0–14 of either innings.

Auction cycle

mini-auction (Dec 2025). Most recent mega-auction was pre-2025; 2026 is a retention/mini-auction year with only marginal squad changes.

Playoffs

Four matches. Qualifier 1 pits the league top two — winner goes direct to the Final. Eliminator is a sudden-death between #3 and #4. Qualifier 2 is Qualifier 1's loser vs the Eliminator winner. Final is a standalone single-match decider.

§ Pre-tournament storylines

Things to know before the first ball

  • RCB enter as defending champions after their maiden title in 2025, under new captain Rajat Patidar — Kohli still in red.
  • Cameron Green joined KKR at ₹25.20 crore, becoming the most expensive overseas signing in IPL history.
  • MS Dhoni's potential farewell season — CSK are rebuilding around Ruturaj Gaikwad after a last-place finish in 2025.
  • Sanju Samson's move from Rajasthan to Chennai after 11 seasons at RR is the most significant Indian transfer.
  • Rishabh Pant (captain) and Shreyas Iyer (captain) swap franchises — Pant now leads LSG, Iyer takes over Punjab.
  • Vaibhav Suryavanshi's second year at Rajasthan — the teenage opener whose 2025 debut was sport's breakout story.
  • The Impact Player rule is retained. 2026 is a mini-auction year — minor churn versus the 2025 mega-auction.
