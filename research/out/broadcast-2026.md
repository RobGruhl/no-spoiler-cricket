# broadcast-2026

### India (TV and Streaming)
**Primary broadcaster: JioStar Network Channels (TV, subscription via cable/DTH) and JioHotstar (streaming, subscription).**  
JioHotstar is the merged entity of JioCinema and Disney+ Hotstar following the Disney-Reliance merger, holding exclusive digital rights for IPL 2023-2027; plans start at Rs 79/month (mobile), Rs 149/month (super, 2 devices).[1][2][7]  
TV via Star Sports channels (Star Sports 1, 1 Hindi, 1 Tamil, etc., including HD).[1][2]

### United States (Indian-Diaspora Audience)
**Primary broadcaster: Willow TV (TV and streaming, subscription).**  
Available via Willow TV app, Fubo, or Sling TV; matches start at 10 AM ET (7:30 PM IST) or 6 AM ET (double-headers).[1][3][5]

### United Kingdom
**Primary broadcaster: Sky Sports Cricket (TV and streaming, subscription).**  
Stream via Sky Go or NOW TV.[1][5]

### Australia
**Primary broadcaster: Fox Sports (TV, subscription) / Fox Cricket.**  
Stream via Kayo Sports.[1][5]

### Canada
**Primary broadcaster: Willow TV / ATN Cricket Plus (TV, subscription).**  
Stream via Willow TV app, ATN Online, Fubo, Sling TV, or YuppTV.[1][3][5][6]

### UAE / Middle East
**Primary broadcaster: beIN Sports (TV, subscription).**  
Stream via beIN CONNECT, StarzPlay, or CricLife app (MENA regions).[1][5]

### Singapore / Southeast Asia
**Primary: YuppTV (select regions, subscription streaming; no dedicated TV listed).**  
Covers rest-of-world areas including Southeast Asia where not specified otherwise; no Singapore-specific free-to-air noted.[5][6]
