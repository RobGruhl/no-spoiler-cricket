# format-rules

**IPL 2026 features 74 matches total: 70 in the league stage (each of 10 teams plays 14 games) plus 4 playoffs (Qualifier 1, Eliminator, Qualifier 2, Final).** [1][3]

### League Stage Structure
- 10 teams divided into **two groups of five**. [1][3][4]
- Each team plays **14 league matches**: home-and-away (twice) against own-group teams (4 matches), and twice against five teams from the opposite group (10 matches). One source notes a reversal to a 2023-like format with once against own group and twice against the other, but the 14-match total and two-group system are consistent across reports.[1][3][4]
- Top 4 teams (by points, regardless of group) advance to playoffs.[4][5]

### Playoff Format
- **Qualifier 1**: League #1 vs #2 (winner to Final).[5]
- **Eliminator**: League #3 vs #4 (winner to Qualifier 2; loser eliminated).[5]
- **Qualifier 2**: Loser of Qualifier 1 vs Eliminator winner (winner to Final).[5]
- **Final**: Qualifier 1 winner vs Qualifier 2 winner.[1][5]
- Playoff venues not specified in available details; Final tentatively at Bengaluru.[1]

### Match and In-Play Rules
- Standard T20 format: 20 overs per innings; highest score wins; ties settled by Super Over; rain-affected matches use Duckworth-Lewis-Stern (DLS) method.[2]
- No confirmed changes to **Impact Player rule**, DRS, saliva ban, or over rates versus 2024/2025; it remains in effect as standard unless announced otherwise.[2]

### Auction Cycle
- IPL 2026 is a **mini/retention year** (not mega-auction).[3]
- Most recent auction: 2025 (pre-2026 season, as mega-auctions occur every 3-4 years).[3]

Tournament runs March 28 to May 31 across 13 venues.[3]
