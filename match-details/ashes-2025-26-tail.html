§ 04 — Storylines

Narratives to watch

  • The Ashes Urn lives with the holders — both sides arrive with the series still in the balance or decided, but the MCG & SCG fixtures carry their own weight regardless of urn status.
  • Pink-ball Tests have shifted the balance in recent Ashes cycles; keep an eye on how the home attack uses Australian conditions.
  • For Indian-origin viewers: these Tests are prime scouting material — India's top order will face many of these bowlers later in the year.
§ 05 — Historical Context

Form book & lore

The Ashes began in 1882 after England lost at home and an obituary declared English cricket dead — 'the body will be cremated and the ashes taken to Australia.' Australia won the 2021-22 and 2023 cycles; the 2025-26 series is the next instalment on Australian soil.

§ 06 — Viewing Notes

When to tune in

Test days run roughly 10:00–17:00 AEDT (three sessions of ~2 hours with breaks). For US east coast viewers, that's an overnight watch — most viewers catch the highlight reels next morning.
